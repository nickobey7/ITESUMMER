Constitution of the University of Guyana Cybersecurity Club

Article 1 - Name of Club

1.1. The club's official name is "UNIVERSITY OF GUYANA CYBERSECURITY CLUB", also known as "UG - Cyber Security Club".

Article 2 - Club Mandate

Vision: To be a highly respected and principled team of skilled cybersecurity practitioners.

Mission: To provide members with knowledge and experience of cybersecurity principles and tools to enhance academic and professional careers.

Strategy: Emphasize self-learning through freely available literature and exercises using FOSS (Free and Open Source Software).

Objectives:

  • Educate members with relevant cybersecurity skills.
  • Discuss issues related to cybersecurity.
  • Share information related to securing information.
  • Participate in local, regional, and international Capture The Flag competitions.
  • Establish linkages with cybersecurity organizations.
Article 3 - Membership

Open to all undergraduate students and faculty of the Computer Science Department at the University of Guyana.

Article 4 - Duties of Members
  • President: Chairs meetings, represents the club, performs administrative duties, sets goals.
  • Vice President: Assists the President, assumes duties in their absence.
  • Secretary: Distributes notices, records minutes, manages member list and social media.
  • Treasurer: Keeps financial records, prepares budgets.
  • Events Coordinator: Organizes programs and events.
  • Club Advisor: Staff member offering guidance; non-voting member of the Executive Committee.
Article 5 - Meetings
  • At least 3 general meetings per semester.
  • Quorum: 1/3 of members, including President or VP and Secretary.
  • One meeting must review the constitution and long-term goals.
  • Executive Committee to be notified of meetings at least 3 days in advance.
Article 6 - Elections/Voting
  • Held annually by September 30th.
  • One vote per member.
  • Simple majority required for success.
Article 7 - Amendments
  • Members must be notified at least 1 week before proposed changes.
  • At least 2/3 of members must approve amendments.
